Georgia Tech was in a close game at the start of the season against NC State. Down 1 in OT with the ball for the final shot. On the final shot though, James Banks, Georgia Tech‚Äôs center drew a shooting foul with under 4 seconds left. Maybe not the most ideal situation with Banks being a career 63.6% FT shooter, but Banks hit both free throws and the Tech basketball team got to celebrate with their new tradition of gas station snacks after an ACC road victory! Watching though, I wondered if NC State was a tough place to shot free throws. Free throws are static, in that, their distance from the basket never change and there is no defender effect on the shooter, unlike normal field goals. What some say does have an effect though, are the fans, their yelling, and the craziness that goes on within the student sections.

Nothing strikes me as NC State being a tough place to shoot free throws, so after having sat on the piece for a little and looking around, I wanted to see if I could quantify if away teams shot worse at certain courts/gyms. Luke Benz has already run some analysis to see if ASU‚Äôs Curtain of Distraction had an effect of away teams when shooting at Arizona State. Rather than look at one team though, I wanted to compare free throws of all away teams to see if some places had multiple years of signs of extra misses. Since I‚Äôm not looking to show causation, I gathered the box scores from the Kaggle competition for the 2019 NCAA tournament which included the 2002-2003 season through the 2017-2018 season (bummer, but if Kaggle does a 2020 competition and last year‚Äôs + this year‚Äôs box scores are on there, I‚Äôll do a follow up!). First, I want to look at year over year trends for away free throw percentages.

Big picture though, I wanted to determine which home teams might have the most help from their court vs the normal Free Throw ability at other away gyms. I opted to run a two proportion z-test on the away team‚Äôs free throw makes/attempts at particular courts vs the combined free throw makes/attempts for the season. My process/code:

#Add Team Names
seasondetaileddataset <- merge(x = seasondetaileddataset, y = Teams, by.x = "HTeamID", by.y = "TeamID", all.x = T)
seasondetaileddataset <- merge(x = seasondetaileddataset, y = Teams, by.x = "ATeamID", by.y = "TeamID", all.x = T)
colnames(seasondetaileddataset)[37] <- "HomeTeamName"
colnames(seasondetaileddataset)[38] <- "AwayTeamName"

#away team summary stats
awayteamftstats <- seasondetaileddataset[, .("AT Free Throws Made (AID)" = sum(AFTM),
                             "AT Free Throws Attempted (AID)" = sum(AFTA)),
                             keyby = .(Season, AwayTeamName)]

#away team free throw stats based on home court
homecourtftstats <- seasondetaileddataset[, .("AT Free Throws Made (HID)" = AFTM,
                                             "AT Free Throws Attempted (HID)" = AFTA,
                                             "AT Free Throw Percentage (HID)" = percent(AFTM/AFTA),
                                             "AwayTeamName" = c(AwayTeamName)),
                                              keyby = .(Season, HomeTeamName)]

#Away Team Season Free Throws with their FT Stats at FT Stats for individual games
ftstats <- homecourtftstats[awayteamftstats, on = c("Season", "AwayTeamName")]

#Free Throw Stats by games at home team gym vs whole season
ftstats <- ftstats[, .("FT Made @ HID" = sum(`AT Free Throws Made (HID)`),
                       "FT Attempted @ HID" = sum(`AT Free Throws Attempted (HID)`),
                       #"AT Free Throw Percentage (HID)" = percent(sum(AFTM)/sum(AFTA)),
                       "FT Made by all AID" = sum(`AT Free Throws Made (AID)`),
                       "FT Attempted by all AID" = sum(`AT Free Throws Attempted (AID)`)),
                        keyby = .(Season, HomeTeamName)]

#proportion test
lesstesttable <- ftstats %>% mutate(pval = NA)
for (x in 1:nrow(lesstesttable)) {
  testy <- prop.test(x = c(lesstesttable$`FT Made @ HID`[x],lesstesttable$`FT Made by all AID`[x]),
                     n = c(lesstesttable$`FT Attempted @ HID`[x],lesstesttable$`FT Attempted by all AID`[x]),
                     alternative = "less",
                     correct = F)
  lesstesttable$pval[x] <- testy$p.value
}

lesstesttable %<>% as.data.table() %>% arrange(pval) %>% filter(pval <= .05)

After filtering for only significant results (those with a p-value < .05) and sorting from most significant to least, we have a winner!

Gonzaga and the University of Texas-Arlington are those two teams where away teams seem to tremble at the free throw line. Again, without looking at causation, I can‚Äôt prove why, but feel free to make your own assumption. My opinion for Gonzaga is that many West Coast Conference (WCC) teams roll into McCarthy Athletic Center while Gonzaga is ranked, and the atmosphere is not like any others in the WCC. They‚Äôve created a dynasty up in the corner of Washington that other non-conference opponents are not ready for. For UTA, I don‚Äôt have a great guess as to why it is tough. 5 of their 7 seasons of significant difference came between 2012-2017 after College Park Center, their home gym, was built. If anyone wants to explain me what‚Äôs going on in Arlington, Texas though, my DMs are open.

Concluding, it‚Äôs not always the blue blood schools that have the toughest gyms to shoot FTs in with loud students and fans yelling obnoxious phrases and waving their arms around behind the backboard. I also thought Syracuse might show up because of the depth of shot inside the Carrier Dome. You must remember though: Free Throws are the same distance and shot on the same height goal at every gym (See the Hoosiers clip where they walk into the Championship gym for the first time).
